What is CPR?

CPR stands for Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation and is emergency life-saving procedure to help someone survive cardiac arrest. This is done by performing chest cxompressions on the victim.

Why is CPR done?

CPR can help people without a pulse the ability to breathe on their ow. Additionally, CPR helps keep blood circulating and delivers oxygen to the victim's body until professional help arrives.

REMINDERS BEFORE DOING CPR

Chain of Survival: this is pivotal!

  • Early help - Call 911 as soon as possible.
  • Early CPR - get on chest as soon as possible.
  • Early defibrillation - find an AED as soon as possible.

S.R.A.B.C

  • Safety - ensure the area you bring the patient to is safe.
  • Response - check for a response.
  • Airway - open the mouth and check the airway.
  • Breathing - check if the patient is breathing.
  • CPR - call for help then proceed to do CPR (ensure it is not interrupted).
